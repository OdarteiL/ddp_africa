DPP Readiness Assessment

A structured 4–8 week audit that tells you exactly where you stand — and what you need to do to comply with EU DPP requirements.

What Is a DPP Readiness Assessment?

Before you can generate a Digital Product Passport, you need to know what data you have, what data you are missing, and where it lives across your supply chain. Most African manufacturers exporting to Europe have never had to document their products at this level of detail — and that gap is exactly what the Readiness Assessment closes.

Our assessment is a structured 4–8 week engagement that maps your entire product data landscape against the specific requirements of the EU delegated acts that apply to your product categories. The output is not a generic report — it is a prioritised, actionable compliance roadmap built around your business.

What We Examine

  • Raw material sourcing documentation across all supply chain tiers
  • Manufacturing process records and location data
  • Carbon footprint and environmental impact measurement capability
  • Repairability and recyclability data availability
  • Existing digital systems, ERPs, and data infrastructure
  • Supplier relationships and data-sharing agreements
  • Current compliance certifications and their EU equivalence

What You Receive

At the end of the assessment you receive a comprehensive gap analysis report and a compliance roadmap. The roadmap is prioritised by deadline urgency — so you know exactly what to tackle first for the February 2027 battery deadline, what can wait for the 2028 textiles wave, and what requires long-term structural change.

  • Full gap analysis report against current EU delegated acts
  • Prioritised compliance roadmap with timelines and owners
  • Technology and process change recommendations
  • Supplier engagement strategy and data collection plan
  • Cost and effort estimates for each remediation action

Who Is This For?

The Readiness Assessment is the right starting point for any African manufacturer or exporter who sells — or plans to sell — physical products in the EU market. It is especially critical for companies in the first-wave categories: batteries, textiles, and electronics, where the 2027 deadline is approaching fast.

It is also the gateway to our DPP Platform subscription. 65% of clients who complete an assessment proceed to platform implementation within 90 days.
