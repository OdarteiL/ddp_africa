Training & Capacity Building

Long-term DPP compliance requires internal knowledge. We build that capability through targeted workshops, pilot projects, and change management support designed for African manufacturers and trade bodies.

Why Internal Capability Matters

Outsourcing your DPP compliance is a smart short-term strategy. But the businesses that will thrive in the post-2030 EU market are those that have built genuine internal understanding of what DPP requires, why it matters, and how to manage it as a routine business process rather than a compliance emergency.

Our Training and Capacity Building programmes are designed to transfer knowledge — not create dependency. We want your team to understand DPP deeply enough to manage it confidently, even as regulations evolve.

Training Programmes

  • DPP Fundamentals Workshop — Half-day introduction to the regulation, timeline, and what it means for your business. Suitable for all staff levels.
  • Operations & Procurement Deep-Dive — Full-day workshop for the teams responsible for supply chain data collection and product documentation.
  • Leadership Briefing — Two-hour executive session covering strategic implications, competitive risk, and investment decisions.
  • DPP Pilot Project — A structured 6-week programme where your team implements DPP compliance for one product line under our guidance, building the skills to replicate it across your portfolio.
  • Trade Association Programme — Group training for industry bodies wanting to equip their members with DPP knowledge. Available in-person and online.

Change Management Support

DPP compliance is not just a technical change — it is an operational one. It requires new processes, new supplier relationships, and new ways of thinking about product data. We provide change management support to help your organisation embed these changes sustainably, including internal playbooks, process documentation, and ongoing coaching for your compliance leads.

For Trade Associations

We work with export promotion agencies, industry associations, and chambers of commerce across Africa to deliver DPP awareness programmes for their members. These can be delivered as standalone events, integrated into existing member services, or licensed as a white-label programme under your brand.
