Batteries & EV

The first mandatory DPP category. EV batteries and industrial batteries must carry a fully compliant Digital Product Passport before they can be sold in the EU — starting February 2027.

Why Batteries Are First

The EU Battery Regulation (EU 2023/1542) established the Digital Product Passport requirement for batteries ahead of all other product categories. The rationale is clear: batteries are critical to the EU's green transition, they contain hazardous materials, and their supply chains — often running through Africa and Asia — have historically lacked transparency.

For African manufacturers and exporters involved in battery production, component supply, or EV assembly, the February 2027 deadline is not a future concern. It is a present one. Preparation needs to start now.

What a Battery DPP Must Contain

  • Battery model and general information (chemistry, capacity, voltage)
  • Carbon footprint declaration across the full lifecycle
  • Recycled content — percentage of cobalt, lithium, nickel, lead from recycled sources
  • Responsible sourcing documentation for critical raw materials
  • State of health and expected lifetime data
  • Hazardous substance information
  • End-of-life information including collection and recycling instructions
  • Supply chain due diligence documentation

The Africa Angle

Africa holds over 70% of the world's cobalt reserves, significant lithium deposits, and growing battery manufacturing capacity. This makes African businesses both critical suppliers to the EU battery market and directly subject to DPP requirements. The Democratic Republic of Congo, Zambia, Zimbabwe, and South Africa are among the countries most immediately affected.

DPP.africa has specific expertise in the African battery supply chain — from artisanal mining documentation to large-scale industrial battery assembly. We understand the unique challenges of documenting supply chains that span multiple countries and involve both formal and informal sector suppliers.
