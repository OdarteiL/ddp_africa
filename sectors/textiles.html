Textiles & Apparel

Africa's largest EU export sector. Every garment, fabric, and footwear item sold in Europe will require a Digital Product Passport from 2028 — documenting material composition, origin, and end-of-life guidance.

The Scale of the Challenge

Textiles and apparel represent one of Africa's most significant export sectors to Europe. Countries including Ethiopia, Morocco, Tunisia, Madagascar, Lesotho, and Kenya have built substantial garment manufacturing industries serving EU brands and retailers. The 2028 DPP deadline affects every one of these manufacturers — and every EU importer sourcing from them.

The textile DPP is among the most data-intensive of all product categories. It requires documentation that most African manufacturers have never had to compile — and that many of their raw material suppliers have never had to provide.

What a Textile DPP Must Contain

  • Fibre composition — type, percentage, and origin of all materials
  • Recycled content percentage and certification
  • Country of origin for each production stage (spinning, weaving, dyeing, assembly)
  • Chemical substances used in production and finishing
  • Durability and repairability information
  • Care and maintenance instructions
  • End-of-life guidance — recyclability, collection points, disposal instructions
  • Carbon footprint across the production lifecycle

The African Textile Opportunity

DPP compliance is not just a compliance burden — it is a competitive opportunity. African manufacturers who can demonstrate transparent, sustainable supply chains will be better positioned to win contracts with EU brands that are under increasing pressure from their own consumers and regulators to source responsibly.

DPP.africa has worked with textile manufacturers across East and North Africa to map their supply chains and build the data infrastructure needed for DPP compliance. We understand the specific challenges of documenting multi-country production processes and working with smallholder cotton and fibre suppliers.
