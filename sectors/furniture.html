Furniture

Wood, metal, and upholstered furniture exported to the EU must carry Digital Product Passports from 2029 — documenting material origin, durability, and disassembly instructions for every piece.

Furniture and Forest Traceability

The furniture DPP requirement is closely linked to the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), which already requires companies to prove that wood-based products are not linked to deforestation. The DPP extends this to a full lifecycle record — covering not just wood origin but all materials, manufacturing processes, and end-of-life guidance.

For African furniture manufacturers, this means building documentation systems that can trace timber from forest to finished product — a significant challenge in markets where informal timber supply chains are common.

What a Furniture DPP Must Contain

  • Material composition — wood species, metal types, fabric content, foam specifications
  • Timber origin and deforestation-free certification (FSC, PEFC, or equivalent)
  • Manufacturing location and process documentation
  • Chemical substances — finishes, adhesives, flame retardants
  • Durability and expected product lifetime
  • Disassembly instructions for repair and end-of-life processing
  • Recyclability information by material component
  • Carbon footprint across the production lifecycle

The African Furniture Opportunity

African furniture manufacturers who can demonstrate sustainable timber sourcing and transparent supply chains are well-positioned to capture growing EU demand for responsibly sourced furniture. The DPP is not just a compliance requirement — it is a marketing tool that lets you prove your sustainability credentials to EU buyers.

DPP.africa works with furniture manufacturers across Southern and East Africa to build timber traceability systems and prepare for the 2029 DPP deadline. We have specific expertise in working with both FSC-certified and non-certified timber supply chains.
