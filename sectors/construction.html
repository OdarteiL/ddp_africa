Construction Materials

Steel, cement, and construction products entering the EU market will require Digital Product Passports from 2030 — documenting environmental performance data, material origin, and end-of-life guidance.

Construction and the Built Environment

The construction sector accounts for approximately 40% of EU energy consumption and 36% of CO2 emissions. The DPP for construction materials is designed to drive transparency about the environmental performance of building products — enabling architects, developers, and regulators to make informed decisions about the materials they specify.

For African exporters of steel, cement, timber, stone, and other construction materials, the 2030 deadline requires building documentation systems that can track environmental performance data across complex industrial supply chains.

What a Construction Materials DPP Must Contain

  • Environmental Product Declaration (EPD) data — carbon footprint, resource use, waste
  • Material composition and origin documentation
  • Manufacturing process and location data
  • Hazardous substance content (particularly for cement and coatings)
  • Structural performance and durability data
  • Recycled content percentage and recyclability information
  • End-of-life deconstruction and recycling guidance
  • Compliance with EU Construction Products Regulation (CPR)

The African Construction Export Opportunity

Africa is a significant exporter of construction materials to Europe — particularly steel from South Africa and Egypt, natural stone from Zimbabwe and South Africa, and timber from Central and West Africa. Manufacturers who can demonstrate low-carbon production and responsible sourcing will be well-positioned as EU construction projects increasingly specify sustainable materials.
