The Regulation

What Is the Digital Product Passport?

The Digital Product Passport (DPP) is a new EU requirement under the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR, EU 2024/1781). Starting in 2027, virtually every physical product sold in the EU must carry a digital record — accessible via QR code, NFC tag, or barcode — containing verified lifecycle data.

This includes raw material origin, carbon footprint, repairability scores, manufacturing location, and end-of-life disposal guidance. The data must be machine-readable, auditable, and maintained for the product's lifetime plus ten years.

Products without a valid DPP cannot legally be placed on the EU market. For African exporters, this is not a distant concern — it is an immediate business risk.

Sectors We Serve

Which Industries Are Affected?

Nearly all physical products sold in the EU will require a DPP. These are the sectors with the earliest deadlines — and the highest stakes for African exporters.

Deadline: Feb 2027

Batteries & EV

EV batteries and industrial batteries are the first mandatory DPP category. African battery manufacturers and component exporters must be ready by February 2027.
Deadline: 2028

Textiles & Apparel

Africa's largest EU export sector. Every garment, fabric, and footwear item must carry a DPP showing material composition, origin, and end-of-life guidance.
Deadline: 2029

Electronics

Consumer electronics, components, and ICT equipment. DPPs must document material sourcing, repairability scores, and recycling instructions for every device.
Deadline: 2029

Furniture

Wood, metal, and upholstered furniture exported to the EU must carry passports documenting material origin, durability, and disassembly instructions.
Deadline: 2030

Chemicals & Plastics

Chemical products and plastic materials require DPPs documenting composition, hazardous substance content, and safe disposal or recycling pathways.
Deadline: 2030

Construction Materials

Steel, cement, and construction products entering the EU market must carry passports with environmental performance data and end-of-life guidance.
